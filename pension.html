This calculator uses actual company balance sheets to determine what percentage of each stock's market capitalisation is backed by zakaatable assets, rather than relying on the commonly-used 25% estimate.

Zakaatable Assets (included)

  • Cash & short-term investments — liquid wealth that is inherently zakaatable
  • Trade receivables — money owed to the company by customers
  • Inventory — goods held for sale, zakaatable as trade goods
  • Other current assets — prepaid items and other short-term liquid assets

Deductible Liabilities (subtracted)

  • Accounts payable — trade debts owed to suppliers
  • Short-term debt — current borrowings (excluding lease obligations)
  • Accrued expenses — wages, interest, and other costs genuinely owed
  • Taxes payable — current tax obligations

Not Deducted (excluded from liabilities)

  • Deferred revenue — money already received, not a real debt
  • Lease liabilities — operational cost, not financial debt in the zakat sense
  • Long-term debt — only current debts are deductible per most scholars
  • Deferred tax liabilities — an accounting construct, not real debt
  • Pension obligations — long-term and uncertain
  • Provisions — uncertain, may never be paid

Formula

Strict = max(Current Assets - Liabilities, 0)
Broad = max(All Liquid Assets - Liabilities, 0)
Assets-Only = All Liquid Assets (no liabilities deducted)
Company Zakaatable % = min(Method Amount / Market Cap × 100, 100%)
Fund Zakaatable % = Σ(Company % × Weight) + Cash Allocation
Zakat = Pension Value × Fund Zakaatable % × 2.5%
